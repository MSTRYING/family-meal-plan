Monday

Breakfast Scrambled eggs on toast
Prep 2 minCook 5 minServes 4Vegetarian

Silky, slow-scrambled eggs on buttered toast. The secret is low heat — keep stirring and pull the pan off heat while the eggs are still slightly wet. They finish cooking in the residual heat.

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 4 tbsp full-cream milk
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 4 slices bread, toasted
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced

Method

  1. 1Whisk eggs and milk together, season well.
  2. 2Melt butter in a non-stick pan over low-medium heat.
  3. 3Add eggs. Stir slowly and continuously, folding from the edges inward.
  4. 4Remove from heat just before fully set. Serve on buttered toast with tomato.

Dinner Vegetable pasta bake
Prep 15 minCook 35 minServes 4

Rich tomato sauce with roasted peppers and onions, mixed with pasta and blanketed in golden melted cheese. One dish, oven finish, crowd-pleaser guaranteed.

CJ add-on: Season 1 chicken breast with salt, pepper and paprika. Pan-fry in oil 6–7 min until cooked, slice and stir into CJ's portion before baking.

Ingredients

  • 300g penne pasta
  • 2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cans chopped tomatoes
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 cup grated cheddar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Method

  1. 1Preheat oven to 200°C. Cook pasta until just al dente. Drain.
  2. 2Fry onion in oil 5 min. Add peppers, cook 3 min. Add garlic and oregano, stir 1 min.
  3. 3Pour in tomatoes, simmer 10 min. Season well.
  4. 4Toss pasta into sauce. Transfer to baking dish. Cover generously with cheese.
  5. 5Bake 20 min until bubbling and golden.

DinnerPotato and chickpea curry with basmati rice
Prep 10 minCook 35 minServes 4

Fragrant, mildly spiced curry with golden potatoes, protein-packed chickpeas and wilted spinach. Make extra — this is even better the next day (Friday lunch!).

CJ add-on: Season 2 chicken thighs with curry powder, salt and oil. Pan-fry 6–7 min per side. Slice and serve on top of CJ's bowl.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans chickpeas, drained
  • 3 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 250ml veg stock
  • 2 cups basmati rice

Method

  1. 1Cook basmati rice per packet (1:1.5 water ratio, 15 min covered).
  2. 2Fry onion in oil 8 min until golden brown. Add garlic and spices, stir 1 min.
  3. 3Add potatoes, tomatoes and stock. Cover and simmer 20 min.
  4. 4Add chickpeas, cook 5 min. Fold in spinach until wilted. Season.
  5. 5Serve curry over rice.

DinnerStir-fried egg noodles with mixed vegetables
Prep 10 minCook 12 minServes 4

Punchy soy-garlic noodles with crunchy vegetables and scrambled egg folded through. High heat is essential — get the wok or pan smoking hot before adding anything.

CJ add-on: Slice 150g beef into thin strips, season with soy sauce and pepper. Fry in a very hot pan 2–3 min until caramelised. Toss through CJ's noodles at the end.

Ingredients

  • 400g egg noodles
  • 4 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (opt)

Method

  1. 1Cook noodles per packet. Drain, rinse under cold water, set aside.
  2. 2Mix soy sauce, sugar and sesame oil in a small bowl.
  3. 3Heat oil in a wok over very high heat until smoking. Fry garlic 30 sec.
  4. 4Add onion and peppers, stir-fry 3 min. Push to the side. Scramble eggs in the gap.
  5. 5Add noodles, spinach and sauce. Toss everything 1–2 min. Serve immediately.

DinnerHomemade pizza with roasted vegetables
Prep 10 minCook 20 minServes 4

Store-bought pizza bases topped with tomato sauce, golden roasted veg and melted cheese. Everyone builds their own — a fun Friday ritual.

CJ add-on: Dice 1 chicken breast, toss in olive oil, salt and paprika, roast at 200°C for 12 min. Scatter over CJ's pizza before the final bake.

Ingredients

  • 4 pizza bases (store-bought)
  • 1 can tomato paste or pizza sauce
  • 2 cups grated mozzarella / cheddar
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt, pepper, chilli flakes

Method

  1. 1Preheat oven to 220°C.
  2. 2Toss peppers, onion and mushrooms in oil and salt. Roast 12 min until edges char.
  3. 3Spread tomato paste over each base. Season with oregano and chilli flakes.
  4. 4Scatter roasted veg over bases. Top generously with cheese.
  5. 5Bake 10–12 min until cheese is golden and bubbling.

BreakfastFluffy pancakes with banana and syrup
Prep 5 minCook 15 minServes 4

Thick, fluffy pancakes stacked high. The key is not overmixing the batter — lumps are good and keep them light and pillowy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1½ cups milk
  • 3 tbsp melted butter
  • 2 bananas, sliced
  • Pancake syrup to serve

Method

  1. 1Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder and salt in a bowl.
  2. 2In another bowl, whisk eggs, milk and melted butter.
  3. 3Pour wet into dry. Stir briefly — leave the batter lumpy, do not overmix.
  4. 4Cook ¼ cup batter per pancake in a lightly buttered pan on medium heat. 2 min per side.
  5. 5Stack, top with banana slices and generous syrup.

CJ add-on: Put the boerewors directly on the grid and cook 15–20 min, turning every few minutes until cooked through and nicely charred. Serve in a roll with mustard or tomato sauce.

  • Salt and pepper

Method

Sunday

Slow, indulgent and nourishing
BreakfastFull vegetarian fry-up
Prep 10 minCook 20 minServes 4

The ultimate Sunday morning. Fried eggs with runny yolks, garlic butter mushrooms, blistered tomatoes and buttered toast. Everything in one big pan in sequence.

Ingredients

  • 8 eggs
  • 4 large tomatoes, halved
  • 500g mushrooms, halved
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tbsp butter + 1 tbsp oil
  • 4 thick bread slices
  • Salt, pepper, fresh thyme (opt)

Method

  1. 1Heat oil in a large pan. Place tomatoes cut-side down. Cook on high 3–4 min until caramelised. Season and set aside.
  2. 2Melt 2 tbsp butter over high heat. Add mushrooms — don't stir 2 min. Add garlic, toss and cook 2 more min. Season with thyme.
  3. 3Reduce heat. Melt remaining butter. Fry eggs gently, covering to set whites.
  4. 4Toast and butter bread. Plate everything together.

DinnerLentil and sweet potato stew with rice
Prep 10 minCook 35 minServes 4

A deeply nourishing stew to close the week. Red lentils melt into a thick, warming base around sweet potato chunks and aromatic spices. Finished with yoghurt and lemon.

CJ add-on: Season 2 chicken thighs with salt, pepper, paprika and olive oil. Bake at 200°C for 30 min until golden. Serve alongside CJ's stew and rice.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups red lentils, rinsed
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 litre veg stock
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups rice
  • Plain yoghurt and lemon to serve

Method

  1. 1Cook rice per packet. Keep warm.
  2. 2Fry onion in oil 6 min until golden. Add garlic and all spices, cook 1 min.
  3. 3Add sweet potatoes, lentils, tomatoes and stock. Stir well.
  4. 4Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer 25 min, stirring occasionally, until lentils dissolve and sweet potato is tender.
  5. 5Season generously. Add a splash of water if too thick.
  6. 6Serve over rice with a spoon of yoghurt and a lemon wedge.

Shopping tips

📱

Check the Makro app first

Download the Makro app before you go. Online prices sometimes differ from in-store, and the app shows current catalogue specials. You can also do click-and-collect at Makro Centurion to save time.

📦

Buy dry goods in bulk

Makro's bulk buying advantage is strongest with rice, oats, pasta, lentils, canned goods and oil. The per-kg price beats smaller packs at other retailers significantly. Stock up for 2 weeks if you can.

🏷️

Check the "More 4 Less" catalogue

Makro's current More 4 Less catalogue (valid until 28 April 2026) has rollback prices on beans, canned goods, margarine and essentials. Check before shopping — can knock R100+ off your basket.

🧂

Buy spices individually

Only buy the spices you don't already have. Cumin, turmeric, paprika and curry powder are used across multiple meals this week. A single set lasts months. Avoid spice kits — they're poor value.

🥩

CJ's meat — keep it lean

CJ's meat add-ons are intentionally small quantities. The 1.5kg chicken pack covers Monday, Tuesday and Friday. Beef mince covers Thursday and Saturday. No waste, minimal cost.

🌿

Fresh veg — buy on the day

Buy most fresh produce at Makro but check quality carefully. If the avocados are very firm, buy them a day or two before you need them so they ripen in time. Mushrooms last about 5 days refrigerated.

♻️

The leftover strategy

Tuesday's curry is intentionally large — CJ takes leftovers in a flask for Wednesday's lunch. Sunday's lentil & sweet potato stew makes 5–6 portions — CJ's flask for Week 2 Monday. Cook once, eat twice. This is how the week stays affordable without eating the same thing twice in a row.

💳

Makro Smart Shopper card

If you have a Makro Smart Shopper card, scan it every visit. Points accumulate quickly on grocery runs of this size. Pair with the Makro app for additional personalised deals.

Vegetarian protein sources this week

Eggs

Complete protein — used 5 days

Eggs provide all 9 essential amino acids. Used in breakfast on Monday, Thursday, Sunday and folded into Wednesday's noodles. Two 18-packs cover the whole week comfortably. Rich in B12, D and choline.

Thursday's rice bowl, Saturday's nachos and Sunday's braai sides. Excellent source of iron and slow-release carbohydrates — a great combination with rice for blood sugar stability.

Cheese, yoghurt and feta appear in almost every day's meals. Critical for B12 intake in a vegetarian diet. The halloumi on Saturday is also a surprisingly complete protein at ~25g per 100g serving.

Key nutrients to watch on a vegetarian diet

Vitamin B12

Covered by dairy and eggs

This week's plan covers B12 through eggs, cheese, yoghurt and milk — all included daily. If you or your fiancée eat dairy and eggs regularly, B12 deficiency is unlikely. Consider a supplement if eggs and dairy are limited in general.

Iron

Lentils, spinach, beans — pair with vitamin C

Plant-based iron (non-haem iron) absorbs better alongside vitamin C. This plan naturally pairs iron-rich lentils and spinach with tomatoes and lemon — both high in vitamin C. This is not accidental.

Omega-3

Consider adding flaxseed or walnuts

This week's plan is low in omega-3, which is the one gap in a vegetarian diet without fish. If you have flaxseed or walnuts at home, stir them into the porridge or yoghurt breakfasts. Worth adding to next week's shop.

Fibre

Excellent — well above average

Lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans, oats and vegetables give this plan a very high fibre content. Most adults eat 15–20g/day; this plan likely hits 35–45g/day. Excellent for gut health and sustained energy levels.

Zinc

Eggs, cheese and legumes provide enough

Vegetarians sometimes run low on zinc. Eggs, cheddar, lentils and chickpeas all contribute meaningfully. Soaking dried lentils or beans before cooking (if using dried) improves zinc absorption by reducing phytates.

Calories and satiety

Well-balanced for active adults

The high protein and fibre combination means everyone should feel genuinely full. If CJ finds portions small without the meat add-ons, increasing rice portions or adding an extra egg at breakfast is the easiest fix.

DinnerMushroom and spinach risotto
Prep 10 minCook 30 minServes 4

Creamy, slowly stirred arborio rice with earthy mushrooms, wilted spinach and a finishing hit of parmesan. A labour of love that pays off every time.

CJ add-on: Pan-fry 200g diced chicken breast in butter for 7 min until golden. Season and stir into CJ's portion of risotto.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups arborio / risotto rice
  • 400g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 litre hot veg stock
  • 60ml white wine or extra stock
  • 50g butter
  • 50g parmesan or cheddar, grated
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Method

  1. 1Fry mushrooms in oil until golden. Set aside.
  2. 2In same pan, soften onion and garlic 5 min. Add rice, stir 2 min.
  3. 3Add wine/stock. Once absorbed, add hot stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly, about 20 min total.
  4. 4Stir in mushrooms, spinach, butter and cheese. Season. Rest 2 min before serving.

DinnerVegetarian shakshuka
Prep 8 minCook 22 minServes 4

Eggs poached directly in a rich, spiced tomato and pepper sauce. Served straight from the pan with bread to mop it all up. One of the best weeknight dinners there is.

CJ add-on: Slice 2 merguez-style beef sausages or regular pork sausages and brown in the pan before adding the tomato sauce. Let them cook through in the sauce.

Ingredients

  • 8 eggs
  • 2 cans chopped tomatoes
  • 2 bell peppers, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes
  • 100g feta, crumbled
  • Crusty bread to serve

Method

  4. 4Crumble feta on top. Serve directly from the pan with crusty bread.

Vegetarian protein sources this week

Ricotta & mozzarella

~12–18g protein per 100g

Week 2 introduces ricotta in stuffed peppers and mozzarella in the caprese pasta. Both are complete protein sources. Ricotta is also rich in calcium and whey proteins that digest quickly — excellent post-activity fuel.

Butter beans

~8g protein per half tin — used 2 days

Thursday's butter bean stew and Monday's minestrone both use butter beans. High in soluble fibre, they slow carbohydrate absorption and keep blood sugar stable for hours. One of the most filling legumes in any diet.

Eggs

Complete protein — 5 breakfasts

Poached eggs on avocado toast, French toast, egg muffins, baked beans and shakshuka — Week 2 is even more egg-forward than Week 1. Each egg provides 6g complete protein with all 9 essential amino acids.

Chickpeas & lentils

~15g protein per cup — used 3 days

Wednesday's chickpea stir-fry, Sunday's coconut curry and a supporting role in Thursday's stew. Pairing legumes with rice across the week provides all essential amino acids even without meat — important for Miguel.

Halloumi

~25g protein per 100g — Saturday

Saturday's veggie burger uses halloumi as the patty alternative — it's one of the highest-protein cheeses available and grills beautifully. At ~R70 for 200g at Makro, it's also excellent value for a protein-dense weekend meal.

Greek yoghurt

~10g protein per 100g — daily snack

Used in Tuesday's smoothie bowls and as a daily snack base. Plain full-fat Greek yoghurt contains more protein than regular yoghurt and provides gut-friendly probiotics. A 500g tub at ~R38 is one of the best value protein foods available.

Week 2 micronutrient highlights

Lycopene boost

Shakshuka — cooked tomatoes maximise absorption

Friday's shakshuka slow-cooks tomatoes in oil — this is the single best way to absorb lycopene, which is associated with reduced cardiovascular risk. The fat in the olive oil acts as a carrier for this fat-soluble antioxidant.

MCT fats

Coconut milk curry — Sunday

Full-fat coconut milk provides medium-chain triglycerides that are metabolised faster than long-chain fats. The coconut curry is satisfying and filling — a great end to the second week with a very different flavour profile to Week 1.

Iron + Vitamin C pairing

Spinach with tomato — used 4 days

Spinach features in Monday's minestrone, Wednesday's stir-fry, Thursday's stew and Friday's shakshuka. Pairing spinach with tomato (vitamin C) significantly increases the absorption of plant-based non-haem iron — critical for a vegetarian diet.

Omega-3 sources

Avocado, walnuts and flaxseed

Tuesday's smoothie bowl and daily avocado toast provide plant-based omega-3 ALA. While ALA conversion to EPA/DHA is limited, the anti-inflammatory benefits of consistent avocado consumption are well-established in the research literature.

What Week 4 adds nutritionally

Tofu

Complete plant protein — ~8g per 100g

Week 4 introduces firm tofu as a protein base in the teriyaki bowl and pad thai. Tofu is one of the few plant foods containing all 9 essential amino acids. It absorbs the flavours of whatever sauce it's cooked in — pressing it before cooking gives a satisfying crispy result.

Chia seeds

Omega-3, calcium and fibre in one spoon

Monday's chia pudding packs ~5g omega-3 ALA, 18% of the daily calcium RDA and 10g fibre per 2-tablespoon serving. Soaking overnight converts the seeds into a gel — easy to digest and very filling. One of the most nutrient-dense plant foods per gram.

Edamame

~11g complete protein per 100g

Edamame (young soybeans) in the sushi bowls provide complete protein plus isoflavones — plant compounds that support bone density and may reduce cardiovascular risk markers. One of the best plant sources of choline, important for brain health.
